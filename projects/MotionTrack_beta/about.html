My science research has focused on using microscopy and data analysis to follow the motion of individual molecules and microscopic particles in order to learn about dynamic processes in nature. This single-particle approach is in fact quite general and with improvements in sensors and computation, there has been effort in many fields to derive insight from the detailed, granular view provided by single object data. What I find most interesting are the similarities between motion at very different scales. Maybe there are commonalities in the underlying physics and perhaps methods developed in one field could find use in another.

These data come from publicly available repositories as well as my own research. Details and links to the data are provided on individual pages. In some cases, outliers and data artifacts are evident, but without knowing the full details of the data collection and processing, I left the data as they were.

The visualizations were made using Processing.js.
